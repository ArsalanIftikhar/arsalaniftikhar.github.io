How I Approach Senior BI Delivery

I run BI work as a product: clear problem framing, governed metrics, performant semantic models, and delivery standards that teams can maintain after handover.

Discovery & requirements

  • Start with operational questions, decision points, and audience-specific pain points.
  • Translate non-technical needs into measurable KPI definitions, with clear expected actions.
  • Align scope early on data availability, timeliness, and acceptable trade-offs.

KPI stewardship

  • Maintain a metric dictionary covering business definition, formula, grain, and owner.
  • Use simple change control for KPI updates so logic changes are visible and approved.
  • Prevent definition drift by tying dashboards back to one agreed metric source.

Data modelling & semantic layer

  • Model around star-schema principles to keep joins predictable and report logic simpler.
  • Design semantic models for performance and maintainability, not only first delivery speed.
  • Apply RLS intentionally and use repeatable DAX design patterns for consistency.

Reliability & quality

  • Run reconciliation checks between source, transformed, and semantic layers.
  • Monitor refresh behaviour and exceptions to catch issues before stakeholder escalation.
  • Validate refresh outputs against known control totals and operational expectations.

Delivery hygiene

  • Maintain concise technical documentation, ERDs, data-flow notes, and runbooks.
  • Use PR-based workflows and peer review habits to reduce avoidable production errors.
  • Promote review culture where logic decisions are explicit and easy to trace.
