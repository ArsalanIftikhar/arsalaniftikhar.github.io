Senior BI Delivery Sequence

A practical sequence for delivering trusted reporting: clear framing, governed KPI logic, semantic model discipline, controlled validation, and reliable handover.

Phase 1

Discovery & framing

What I need

  • Priority business decisions and current reporting pain points.
  • Source inventory, data owners, and refresh expectations.

What I produce

  • Agreed scope, outcomes, and non-goals.
  • High-level source-to-semantic data flow.

Risk reduced

  • Prevents scope drift and late discovery of missing data.
Phase 2

KPI definition & stewardship

What I need

  • KPI definitions, grain, owners, and policy/finance rules.
  • Agreement on inclusions, exclusions, and action expectations.

What I produce

  • Concise KPI dictionary with logic and lineage notes.
  • Lightweight change-control log for definition updates.

Risk reduced

  • Reduces metric drift and conflicting report interpretations.
Phase 3

Data modelling & semantic layer

What I need

  • Confirmed KPI logic and required reporting cuts.
  • Business key/grain alignment across sources.

What I produce

  • Maintainable model structure (fact/dimension patterns, conformed keys, date strategy).
  • Documented semantic relationships and measure ownership standards.

Risk reduced

  • Limits logic sprawl and improves consistency across reports.
Phase 4

Validation & controls

What I need

  • Trusted baselines or control totals for comparison.
  • Exception thresholds and escalation owners.

What I produce

  • Reconciliation checks from source to transformed to semantic output.
  • Control routines for refresh failures, threshold breaches, and issue triage.

Risk reduced

  • Prevents silent data issues and improves confidence in reported metrics.
Phase 5

Performance review

What I need

  • Real user journeys and high-cost report interactions.
  • Baseline timings from model checks and DAX Studio traces.

What I produce

  • Targeted tuning notes for model, measures, and visual patterns.
  • Documented trade-offs so optimisations remain maintainable.

Risk reduced

  • Reduces slow, fragile reports and late-stage performance surprises.
Phase 6

Release, handover & iteration

What I need

  • UAT sign-off, support ownership, and production-readiness checks.
  • Clear cadence for post-release review and enhancements.

What I produce

  • Release notes, runbook, and handover pack with support paths.
  • Prioritised improvement backlog based on value and risk.

Risk reduced

  • Reduces key-person dependency and improves BAU reliability after go-live.
