BI, Automation & Data Product Delivery Approach

A practical delivery sequence for turning messy operational needs into governed BI, automation and data products: clear discovery, trusted KPI logic, fit-for-purpose modelling, validation controls, user adoption, documentation and iteration.

Phase 1

Discovery & framing

What I need

  • Priority business decisions, operational pain points and current workflow/reporting friction.
  • Source inventory, process owners, users, refresh needs and adoption constraints.

What I produce

  • Agreed scope, outcomes, non-goals and success measures.
  • High-level source-to-output/workflow map.

Risk reduced

  • Prevents scope drift, unclear ownership and late discovery of missing data/process constraints.
Phase 2

KPI, workflow & data definition

What I need

  • KPI definitions, business rules, process steps, ownership points and exception paths.
  • Agreement on inclusions, exclusions, approval logic and action expectations.

What I produce

  • KPI/process dictionary with logic, grain, owners and lineage notes.
  • Lightweight change-control log for definitions, workflow rules or reporting changes.

Risk reduced

  • Reduces metric drift, workflow ambiguity and conflicting interpretations.
Phase 3

Data modelling, capture design & semantic layer

What I need

  • Confirmed KPI/process logic, required reporting cuts and capture fields.
  • Business key/grain alignment across source systems, apps and reporting outputs.

What I produce

  • Maintainable model structure, capture fields, relationships, date strategy and reusable semantic logic.
  • Documented data flows from source/capture through transformation and reporting.

Risk reduced

  • Limits logic sprawl and prevents downstream reporting problems caused by poor capture or unclear grain.
Phase 4

Validation & controls

What I need

  • Trusted baselines, control totals, expected record counts and exception thresholds.
  • Escalation owners for failures, data-quality issues or approval bottlenecks.

What I produce

  • Reconciliation checks from source/capture to transformed and semantic output.
  • Control routines for refresh failures, validation breaches, workflow exceptions and issue triage.

Risk reduced

  • Prevents silent data issues and improves confidence in operational outputs before users rely on them.
Phase 5

Adoption, usability & performance review

What I need

  • Real user journeys, frontline/manager workflows and high-cost report interactions.
  • Usage patterns, feedback, performance bottlenecks and adoption blockers.

What I produce

  • Usability improvements, targeted model/report tuning, simplified user flows and documented trade-offs.
  • Adoption notes or feedback backlog prioritised by business value and friction.

Risk reduced

  • Reduces slow, fragile or unused solutions and improves practical adoption.
Phase 6

Release, handover & iteration

What I need

  • UAT sign-off, support ownership, documentation expectations and post-release review cadence.
  • Clear handover routes for analysts, managers or junior contributors.

What I produce

  • Release notes, runbook, handover pack, support paths and prioritised improvement backlog.
  • Where relevant, mentoring notes or delivery standards that help junior contributors maintain quality.

Risk reduced

  • Reduces key-person dependency and improves BAU reliability after go-live.
