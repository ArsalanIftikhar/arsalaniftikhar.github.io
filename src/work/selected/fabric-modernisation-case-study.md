<section class="case-study-section" aria-labelledby="reporting-challenge-heading">
  <div class="case-study-section__heading">
    <p class="eyebrow">Reporting challenge</p>
    <h2 id="reporting-challenge-heading">Recurring reports needed one trusted version of performance</h2>
  </div>
  <div class="case-study-section__body">
    <p>Operational, workforce and financial reporting drew from multiple business sources. The work was not only to publish dashboards, but to make sure recurring outputs used consistent definitions, reliable data logic and clear explanations when performance moved.</p>
    <p>Some outputs could look plausible while still being wrong because a filter, date grain, business rule or source extract had changed. That made reconciliation and validation essential before reports were used in senior performance discussions.</p>
    <p>The reporting also needed to connect numbers back to operational causes. Stakeholders needed to understand whether a result pointed to productivity, planning, workload, downtime, source-data quality or another operational driver.</p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="case-study-section" aria-labelledby="reporting-model-heading">
  <div class="case-study-section__heading">
    <p class="eyebrow">Reporting model</p>
    <h2 id="reporting-model-heading">A source-to-decision workflow for operational BI</h2>
  </div>
  <div class="case-study-section__body">
    <p>I developed Power BI reports and semantic models supported by SQL extraction, Power Query shaping and DAX measures. The model work included date logic, dimensional structure, reusable calculations and reporting logic that could support multiple business functions without redefining the same metric in every report.</p>
    <p>The aim was to create a reporting layer that was easier to maintain and easier to challenge. When a number changed, the logic could be traced through source data, transformation steps, semantic model definitions and final report visuals.</p>
    <ol class="case-study-workflow" aria-label="Source-to-decision workflow">
      <li>
        <span class="case-study-workflow__number">01</span>
        <strong>Operational and business sources</strong>
        <p>Workforce, operational and financial inputs supplied the recurring reporting base.</p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <span class="case-study-workflow__number">02</span>
        <strong>SQL and Power Query transformation</strong>
        <p>Extract, clean, combine and shape data into report-ready structures.</p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <span class="case-study-workflow__number">03</span>
        <strong>Semantic model and metric logic</strong>
        <p>Define relationships, date logic, grain and DAX measures in a controlled layer.</p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <span class="case-study-workflow__number">04</span>
        <strong>Power BI reporting</strong>
        <p>Publish operational and leadership views that explain performance at the right level.</p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <span class="case-study-workflow__number">05</span>
        <strong>Stakeholder review</strong>
        <p>Review outputs, definitions and drivers with operational and senior stakeholders.</p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <span class="case-study-workflow__number">06</span>
        <strong>Operational action</strong>
        <p>Use the reporting to investigate issues, prioritise action and track improvement.</p>
      </li>
    </ol>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="case-study-section" aria-labelledby="kpi-stakeholder-heading">
  <div class="case-study-section__heading">
    <p class="eyebrow">KPI definition</p>
    <h2 id="kpi-stakeholder-heading">Metric logic started with the management question</h2>
  </div>
  <div class="case-study-section__body">
    <p>I worked with stakeholders to translate reporting requirements into controlled KPI and metric-definition logic. The first step was understanding the decision being supported, then agreeing the data grain, business rules, exclusions and comparison periods that made the metric useful.</p>
    <p>Where competing definitions existed, I helped clarify the difference between operational, financial and workforce views so the report could explain the definition being used rather than hiding it. I also explained results and drivers in plain language so non-technical stakeholders could connect report outputs to management action.</p>
    <div class="case-study-definition-grid" aria-label="KPI definition and stakeholder delivery">
      <article class="case-study-definition">
        <h3>Decision question</h3>
        <p>Start from the action or performance question the report needs to support.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="case-study-definition">
        <h3>Metric grain</h3>
        <p>Agree whether the measure is viewed by day, week, shift, function, area or another operational grain.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="case-study-definition">
        <h3>Definition control</h3>
        <p>Document business rules, exclusions and calculation logic so repeated reporting stays consistent.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="case-study-definition">
        <h3>Stakeholder explanation</h3>
        <p>Turn the result into a clear operational story, including drivers, caveats and next action.</p>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="case-study-section case-study-section--prominent" aria-labelledby="validation-controls-heading">
  <div class="case-study-section__heading">
    <p class="eyebrow">Validation and controls</p>
    <h2 id="validation-controls-heading">Trust depended on reconciliation, not report polish</h2>
  </div>
  <div class="case-study-section__body">
    <p>I investigated incorrect or inconsistent outputs by tracing the path from report visuals back through measures, transformations and source data. That included reconciling reports to source systems, reviewing transformation logic, comparing results across reports and periods, and challenging outputs that looked plausible but did not match operational reality.</p>
    <p>The control approach was practical: keep metric definitions reusable, review unusual movements, compare repeated outputs with prior periods, and use stakeholder review to confirm whether a data issue or a real operational driver explained the result.</p>
    <div class="case-study-control-grid" aria-label="Reporting validation controls">
      <article class="case-study-control">
        <h3>Source reconciliation</h3>
        <p>Checked report totals and key measures back to the underlying source extracts where the output was disputed or high impact.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="case-study-control">
        <h3>Transformation review</h3>
        <p>Reviewed SQL, Power Query and model logic to find filter, join, grain or date-rule issues.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="case-study-control">
        <h3>Report comparison</h3>
        <p>Compared outputs across reports, periods and business views to spot drift or duplicated logic.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="case-study-control">
        <h3>Metric reuse</h3>
        <p>Used reusable DAX measures and semantic model logic to reduce conflicting versions of the same calculation.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="case-study-control">
        <h3>Unexpected outputs</h3>
        <p>Investigated results that looked valid visually but did not fit the operational context or source baseline.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="case-study-control">
        <h3>Traceability</h3>
        <p>Kept definitions and reporting logic understandable enough for review, handover and repeat investigation.</p>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="case-study-section" aria-labelledby="automation-heading">
  <div class="case-study-section__heading">
    <p class="eyebrow">Automation</p>
    <h2 id="automation-heading">Recurring manual reporting was standardised into repeatable workflows</h2>
  </div>
  <div class="case-study-section__body">
    <p>I automated recurring reporting processes where repeated manual steps created effort, delay or error risk. The automation work covered patterns such as multi-file ingestion, data cleaning, calculations, visual refresh, report distribution and standardised repeatable workflows.</p>
    <p>The value was not automation for its own sake. The important shift was moving recurring outputs away from fragile manual handling and toward logic that could be rerun, reviewed and maintained consistently.</p>
    <div class="case-study-metric-grid" aria-label="Reporting automation metric">
      <article class="case-study-metric">
        <span class="case-study-metric__value">10+</span>
        <h3>Recurring processes automated</h3>
        <p>Automation standardised repeated reporting steps across ingestion, cleaning, calculation, refresh and distribution activity.</p>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
