Floor Walk Power App Case Study

I helped replace manual floor-walk capture and double handling with a governed mobile-first workflow. This improved issue logging consistency, clarified ownership, and made recurring operational risks easier to review and escalate.

Original friction

Paper/spreadsheet capture created duplicate handling, inconsistent labels, and fragmented accountability.

What changed

Floor-walk observations moved to standardised mobile capture with governed fields and repeatable reporting.

My role / ownership

Senior BI Analyst delivering data/governance design, reporting model structure, and operational rollout support.

Measured impact

Estimated 4–8 staff hours/day saved from reduced manual collation and less rework in reporting preparation.

Problem

Before digitisation, floor-walk issues were captured in mixed formats and then re-entered for reporting. This manual chain introduced double handling, delayed visibility, and variation in categories, location names, and action-owner fields.

As a result, senior review discussions often spent time reconciling wording and ownership rather than addressing recurring operational issues. The business needed practical, low-friction capture that produced analysis-ready data from the start.

My role / ownership

  • Defined the core capture structure needed for reliable trend reporting (category, location, severity context, owner, status, timestamps).
  • Set governance guardrails for controlled values and naming consistency to reduce interpretation drift.
  • Designed the reporting-ready shaping approach in Power Query and aligned outputs to Power BI review requirements.
  • Supported rollout feedback loops with operations teams to keep the process practical for warehouse usage.
  • Owned BI-side reporting logic and review views used in recurring operational and senior conversations.

Solution

The delivery used a simple four-part operating pattern: Power Apps for capture, SharePoint List for governed storage, Power Query for shaping/validation, and Power BI for repeatable operational review. The emphasis was reliability and adoption over feature-heavy complexity.

Why this design worked

  • Power Apps: low-friction mobile-first input suited to warehouse floor usage, improving adoption during live operations.
  • SharePoint List: simple governed storage with controlled fields and practical administration for business teams.
  • Power Query: consistent shaping and validation so reporting inputs were standardised before semantic use.
  • Power BI: repeatable operational review of recurring issues, ownership trends, and escalation evidence over time.

Decision outcomes enabled

  • Easier senior review of recurring issues by area/category with fewer debates about data consistency.
  • Clearer ownership/accountability because issue records retained explicit owner and status fields.
  • Evidence-backed escalation using trend and recurrence patterns instead of isolated anecdotes.
  • Faster operational follow-up because teams could focus on actions rather than manual data preparation.

Results / impact

  • Estimated 4–8 staff hours/day saved by reducing double handling and manual collation steps.
  • Improved consistency of issue logging through standardised categories and location conventions.
  • Stronger governance of floor-walk data through required fields, controlled values, and timestamped records.
  • More reliable operational reporting foundation for recurring review, prioritisation, and continuous-improvement follow-through.

Next steps

  • Add threshold-based alerts for high-frequency repeat issues by category/location.
  • Introduce SLA-style follow-up measures for higher-severity findings.
  • Review category taxonomy quarterly to keep labels aligned with operational reality.
