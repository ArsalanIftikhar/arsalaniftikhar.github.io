Floor Walk Digitisation with Power Apps & Power BI

Digitised a manual inventory/floor-walk process into a governed Power Apps and Power BI workflow, reducing double handling, improving issue visibility and saving an estimated 4–8 staff hours/day across c.30 users and 3 shifts.

Original friction

Manual floor-walk capture created double handling, inconsistent categories, delayed reporting visibility and fragmented accountability.

What changed

A governed Power Apps → SharePoint → Power Query → Power BI workflow standardised issue capture, evidence, ownership and operational review.

My role / ownership

Owned the BI-side data structure, reporting model, governance rules, review views and rollout feedback loops with operations.

Measured impact

Estimated 4–8 staff hours/day saved across c.30 users and 3 shifts by reducing manual collation, re-keying and reporting preparation effort.

Problem

Floor-walk issues were captured inconsistently across paper, notes and spreadsheets. Before the information could be used for reporting, it often had to be re-entered, manually cleaned or interpreted by someone who understood the original wording.

Categories, locations, owners and statuses were inconsistent, which meant senior review time could be spent reconciling wording and ownership rather than acting on recurring issues. The business needed low-friction capture that worked in a warehouse environment and produced analysis-ready data from the start.

My role / ownership

  • Defined the core capture fields needed for reliable trend reporting: category, location, owner, status, timestamp, issue detail and evidence/photo reference where applicable.
  • Set governance guardrails for controlled values and naming consistency across categories, locations, owners and statuses.
  • Designed the Power Query shaping and validation approach to reduce manual clean-up before reporting.
  • Aligned the output structure to Power BI review requirements for recurring operational and senior conversations.
  • Built or supported BI-side review views covering recurring issues, ownership, status and follow-through patterns.
  • Worked with operations to keep the workflow practical for warehouse usage and supported feedback loops after rollout.

Solution

The delivery used a simple operating pattern designed for adoption and reporting consistency rather than unnecessary complexity:

  • Power Apps: mobile/frontline issue capture with a structured form for floor-walk observations.
  • SharePoint List: governed storage with controlled fields and practical administration for business teams.
  • Power Query: shaping, cleaning, validation and reporting-ready outputs to reduce manual preparation.
  • Power BI: recurring review by area, category, shift, owner/status and follow-through.

Governance and reporting controls

The controls were designed to reduce interpretation drift and improve confidence in issue trends before records reached recurring review.

Controlled categories

Standardised issue labels so recurring themes could be compared across teams and time periods.

Location conventions

Consistent location naming reduced manual grouping and made area-level trends easier to review.

Owner/status fields

Required ownership and status fields where appropriate supported follow-through and accountability.

Timestamped records

Created a clearer timeline for issue capture, recurrence and follow-up activity.

Power Query shaping

Reduced manual clean-up by standardising reporting inputs before semantic/reporting use.

Reporting-ready structure

Aligned capture fields to recurring operational review needs by area, category, shift and owner/status.

Why this design worked

  • Power Apps: low-friction mobile-first input suited to warehouse floor usage, improving adoption during live operations.
  • SharePoint List: simple governed storage with controlled fields and practical administration for business teams.
  • Power Query: consistent shaping and validation so reporting inputs were standardised before semantic use.
  • Power BI: repeatable operational review of recurring issues, ownership trends and escalation evidence over time.

Decision outcomes enabled

  • Easier senior review of recurring issues by area/category/shift.
  • Clearer ownership and follow-through on actions.
  • Evidence-backed escalation using trend and recurrence patterns.
  • Faster weekly/monthly review preparation because teams spent less time manually collating records.
  • Better basis for continuous-improvement prioritisation.

Results / impact

  • Estimated 4–8 staff hours/day saved across c.30 users and 3 shifts by reducing double handling, manual collation and reporting preparation.
  • Improved consistency of issue logging through standardised categories, locations, owners and statuses.
  • Increased confidence in floor-walk reporting by making capture more structured and analysis-ready.
  • Improved visibility of recurring operational issues by area, category and shift.
  • Supported evidence-based escalation and follow-through on recurring operational actions.

What this demonstrates

  • Power Platform delivery: turning an operational process into a governed capture workflow.
  • BI governance: designing capture fields with downstream reporting and KPI consistency in mind.
  • Operational adoption: building around frontline/warehouse usability.
  • Commercial awareness: reducing manual admin and improving action visibility.
  • Stakeholder delivery: translating messy operational needs into a repeatable reporting process.

Next steps / what I would improve

  • Threshold-based alerts for high-frequency repeat issues by category/location.
  • SLA-style follow-up measures for high-priority findings.
  • Better closed-loop tracking from issue capture to action completion.
  • Periodic review of category/location taxonomy to keep reporting aligned to operational reality.
  • Optional manager/team views for accountability and coaching.

Scope note: this case study focuses on process digitisation, governed capture and reporting visibility. It does not claim fully automated issue resolution or direct financial savings.
