Self-Service Kiosk Case Study

I delivered a self-service reporting experience for frontline colleagues, making operational performance information easier to access without relying on repeated manual report requests.

Business need

Frontline colleagues needed faster access to operational reporting and clearer performance visibility during live shifts.

What changed

Self-service access reduced dependency on ad-hoc reporting support and made key operational views easier to consume.

My role

BI/data delivery across requirements, report structure, adoption support and practical rollout considerations.

Adoption signal

Reached c.200 report views/day from frontline colleagues using the self-service reporting route.

Problem

Operational reporting was valuable, but access was too dependent on manual requests and individual report consumers. Frontline colleagues needed a simpler way to reach trusted reporting during the working day, while managers needed reporting usage to scale without adding avoidable administration.

Solution

  • Created a self-service reporting access point focused on frontline usability and repeatable operational views.
  • Structured report content around practical questions colleagues needed to answer during live operations.
  • Supported adoption by keeping the experience simple, visible and aligned to existing ways of working.
  • Maintained a governed reporting mindset so self-service access did not weaken metric consistency.

Results / impact

  • Built self-service reporting adopted by frontline colleagues, reaching c.200 report views/day.
  • Reduced dependency on repeated manual report requests by making trusted views easier to access directly.
  • Improved operational reporting adoption by matching delivery to real frontline usage patterns.
  • Strengthened the portfolio of governed reporting products available to operational teams.

What this demonstrates

  • Product-minded BI delivery: turning reporting into an accessible operational product, not just a dashboard.
  • Stakeholder-led adoption: designing around frontline behaviour and day-to-day operational needs.
  • Commercial awareness: reducing avoidable admin and improving access to information at scale.
