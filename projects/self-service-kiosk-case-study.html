Self-Service Kiosk for Frontline Performance & Downtime Capture

Built a self-service kiosk using Raspberry Pi, VuePilot, Power BI, Power Apps and Power Automate, enabling frontline colleagues to check WLM/performance information and submit downtime incidents without waiting for manager availability.

Business need

Frontline colleagues needed faster access to WLM/performance information and a structured route to submit downtime incidents without relying on manager availability.

What changed

A dedicated kiosk interface combined Power BI reporting, embedded Power Apps input capture and Power Automate approval notifications.

My role

Designed and delivered the workflow across reporting, input capture, automation, deployment considerations, adoption support and iteration.

Adoption signal

Reached c.200 report views/day over a 2-month period, showing strong frontline engagement with the self-service route.

Problem

Frontline colleagues relied on manager availability to check WLM/performance ratings and resolve simple performance questions during live operations. That created avoidable delays, especially when managers were away from desks, supporting another area, or focused on immediate operational issues.

Downtime capture also needed a more structured route. Informal or manager-dependent processes created friction, made follow-up less consistent, and limited the usefulness of downtime information for reporting. The objective was not just another dashboard; it was a usable operational access point simple enough for a warehouse/frontline environment.

Solution

  • Created a dedicated kiosk interface for frontline self-service performance checks and downtime submission.
  • Used Power BI for WLM/performance and operational views that colleagues could access directly from the kiosk.
  • Embedded a Power App to capture downtime incidents in a structured, repeatable format.
  • Connected Power Automate to trigger manager approval/rejection notifications after downtime submission.
  • Used Raspberry Pi and VuePilot to support deployed screen management, kiosk display control and report rotation.
  • Kept the interface simple and task-led so it could work in a live warehouse/frontline setting.

5SharePoint storage

Triggers approval notifications.

7Manager approval/rejection

Supports controlled review.

8Updated reporting view

Feeds downtime visibility and review.

My role

  • Designed the kiosk workflow around frontline usage patterns and manager availability constraints.
  • Connected reporting, data capture and approval flow into a single operational access route.
  • Configured Power BI, Power Apps and Power Automate components for the self-service workflow.
  • Supported deployment and adoption, including practical device/display considerations.
  • Iterated based on operational feedback so the workflow stayed usable in live conditions.
  • Balanced usability with governed reporting, consistent data capture and metric trust.

Results / impact

  • Reached c.200 report views/day over a 2-month period.
  • Reduced dependency on manager availability for performance checks and downtime submission.
  • Improved frontline access to trusted operational information.
  • Created a structured route for downtime incident capture.
  • Demonstrated practical adoption of self-service reporting in a frontline environment.
  • Strengthened the operational reporting product set beyond conventional dashboards.

What this demonstrates

  • Product-minded BI delivery: turning reporting into a usable operational product.
  • Power Platform automation: connecting Power BI, Power Apps and Power Automate into an operational workflow.
  • Frontline adoption: designing around real warehouse usage patterns.
  • Practical technical integration: Raspberry Pi, VuePilot and the Microsoft stack working together.
  • Governance mindset: making self-service access useful without weakening metric consistency.

What I would improve next

  • Better telemetry on kiosk usage by screen/report.
  • More structured downtime categories and validation.
  • Manager-facing approval queue improvements.
  • Stronger feedback loop from submitted downtime to coaching/action tracking.
  • Cleaner device and content management if scaled across more screens.

Scope note: this case study focuses on the kiosk/product workflow and adoption signal. It does not claim full downtime-management automation, enterprise-wide rollout, or quantified financial savings.
