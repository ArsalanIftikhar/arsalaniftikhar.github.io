GXO Fabric Modernisation (Flagship Case Study)

I owned a defined subset of dataset modernisation within a wider GXO migration workstream. The result was more reliable reporting foundations, clearer KPI logic, and stronger handover support for BAU operations.

What changed

Legacy reporting logic was consolidated into clearer Fabric-aligned layers with reconciliation checks and stronger semantic-model discipline.

Why it mattered

Operations and leadership needed trusted metrics, less logic sprawl across reports, and dependable day-to-day reporting continuity.

What I owned directly

A scoped subset of datasets/tables across Pre-Advice, Orders, and Inventory Transactions, plus controls, documentation, and performance pass activities.

Role & scope boundary

Senior MI Analyst contributor in a broader migration programme. I delivered within defined ownership, and collaborated on cross-stream decisions.

Overview / context

This work sat within an enterprise Fabric migration and modernisation effort in a live logistics environment. The objective was not a cosmetic rebuild; it was to improve reporting trust, reduce duplicated logic, and establish supportable BI products that could run reliably in BAU.

My focus area was a defined subset of core operational domains where reporting consistency and ownership were critical: Pre-Advice, Orders, and Inventory Transactions.

Problem

Legacy assets had accumulated transformation and KPI logic in multiple places. That created metric drift, duplicated logic, and avoidable reconciliation overhead. Teams could get different answers to the same question depending on which report logic they touched.

The migration therefore needed to do more than move code. It had to enforce clearer semantic ownership, improve comparability across sites, and provide controls that supported operational confidence during transition.

My ownership within the wider workstream

  • Owned delivery for a defined subset of Fabric dataset/table work across Pre-Advice, Orders, and Inventory Transactions.
  • Maintained ERDs and data-flow documentation for the scoped entities, joins, grain, and KPI-relevant definitions.
  • Implemented and maintained reconciliation checks for source-to-transform-to-semantic consistency on critical measures.
  • Performed targeted semantic model and DAX performance reviews, documenting findings and practical trade-offs.
  • Contributed runbook/handover artefacts to improve supportability and reduce single-person dependency.

I contributed this ownership inside a multi-person migration programme and did not own all workstream decisions end-to-end.

Approach / solution

  • Aligned scoped tables to layered Fabric patterns so ingestion, transformation, and semantic responsibilities were clearer.
  • Used PySpark and SQL transformations to standardise typing, deduplicate records, and enforce consistent business grain.
  • Supported semantic-layer consistency by keeping measures and relationships maintainable and audit-friendly.
  • Embedded reconciliation and exception routines so migration confidence could be validated before downstream usage.
  • Produced practical documentation/runbooks for handover, issue triage, and repeatable BAU support routines.

Grounded example: PySpark standardisation + de-duplication

typed_df = (
    source_df
    .select(
        F.col("site_id").cast("string").alias("site_id"),
        F.to_date("event_date").alias("event_date"),
        F.col("entity_key").cast("string").alias("entity_key"),
        F.trim(F.upper(F.col("category"))).alias("category_std"),
        F.col("status").cast("string").alias("status"),
        F.col("source_updated_ts").cast("timestamp").alias("source_updated_ts")
    )
    .filter(
        F.col("site_id").isNotNull() &
        F.col("event_date").isNotNull() &
        F.col("entity_key").isNotNull()
    )
)

window_spec = Window.partitionBy("site_id", "event_date", "entity_key").orderBy(F.col("source_updated_ts").desc_nulls_last())

deduped_df = (
    typed_df
    .withColumn("rn", F.row_number().over(window_spec))
    .filter(F.col("rn") == 1)
    .drop("rn")
    .withColumn("record_hash", F.sha2(F.concat_ws("|", "site_id", "event_date", "entity_key", "category_std", "status"), 256))
)

(deduped_df.write.mode("overwrite").format("delta").saveAsTable("lakehouse.silver_ops_events"))

Why this design / trade-offs

  • Layered model over quick patching: slightly higher up-front effort, but materially lower logic sprawl and easier support over time.
  • Controlled standardisation over one-off local rules: less short-term flexibility, but better cross-site comparability and KPI governance.
  • Incremental migration over big-bang cutover: slower in places, but reduced disruption risk for operational reporting.
  • Documented routines over tacit knowledge: additional delivery overhead, but stronger handover quality and lower key-person risk.

Decision outcomes enabled

  • More consistent KPI interpretation across operational and leadership reporting conversations.
  • Faster issue triage through clearer lineage and reconciliation checkpoints.
  • Higher confidence in recurring performance packs because semantic logic was easier to validate and explain.
  • Improved change confidence for future enhancements due to clearer ownership and runbook guidance.

Results / impact

  • Reduced duplicated reporting logic by consolidating key transformation/metric handling into a governed Fabric-aligned structure.
  • Improved confidence in metrics through reconciliation checks and clearer semantic definitions in owned scope areas.
  • Strengthened supportability through ERD/data-flow documentation, runbook updates, and explicit escalation guidance.
  • Provided more reliable reporting foundations for BAU operations while the wider migration continued.

Next steps / what I’d improve

  • Expand control coverage from current critical metrics to a broader quality-monitoring set with prioritised thresholds.
  • Formalise semantic-model review cadence around usage telemetry and DAX query patterns, not only ad-hoc tuning cycles.
  • Add lightweight automated documentation generation for key lineage and business definitions to reduce manual maintenance effort.
