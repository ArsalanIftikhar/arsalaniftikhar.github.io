GXO Fabric Modernisation Case Study

Role: Senior MI Analyst (GXO Logistics) | Focus: BI modernisation, semantic model performance, and reporting reliability.

Context / problem

Legacy reporting workflows had grown uneven over time, with inconsistent KPI definitions and duplicated business logic across multiple reporting assets. The objective was to modernise these datasets into Microsoft Fabric while reducing ambiguity in definitions and improving maintainability.

Stakeholders & constraints

  • Operational leaders requiring reliable day-to-day performance reporting.
  • MI/BI users needing trusted definitions and better report responsiveness.
  • Constraints included continuity of reporting, controlled rollout, and non-confidential working practices suitable for enterprise governance.

Approach

  • Introduced clear layering from source-aligned extracts to transformed analytical tables.
  • Built and documented transformations in repeatable patterns.
  • Produced ERDs and data-flow documentation to clarify dependencies and ownership.
  • Implemented reconciliation checks between source, transformed, and semantic outputs.

Semantic model + DAX performance pass

The semantic layer was reviewed for relationship clarity, reusable measure structure, and DAX efficiency. DAX Studio was used to evaluate query behavior and support measure tuning decisions before release.

Outcomes

  • Improved reporting reliability through clearer data flow and reconciliation controls.
  • Faster path from data updates to stakeholder insight through more consistent modelling patterns.
  • Reduced manual effort by removing duplicated logic and improving delivery documentation.
