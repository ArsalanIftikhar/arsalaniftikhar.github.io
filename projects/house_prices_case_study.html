House Price Prediction

Machine learning project to predict house sale prices using regression models

Project Type

Personal Project

Focus

Machine Learning

Core Technologies

Python, Pandas, Scikit-learn

Dataset

Ames, Iowa Housing

Project Mission

To build a regression model that can accurately predict the sale price of a house based on its features. The dataset is rich, with 79 variables describing everything from the number of bedrooms to the floor space.

Modelling & Evaluating

I then went on to build and compare several regression models, utilising the function below to streamline the process of training and evaluating each model.

def run_model(model):
    model.fit(X_train, y_train)
    y_preds = model.predict(X_test)
    MAE = mean_absolute_error(y_test, y_preds)
    RMSE = np.sqrt(mean_squared_error(y_test, y_preds))
    print(f'MAE: {MAE}, RMSE:{RMSE}')

Upon evaluation, the Gradient Boosting Regressor consistently delivered the best performance. After tuning, it achieved a Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) that was approximately 12.5% of the mean sale price, indicating a high level of accuracy.

Beyond the Project

This project gave me first-hand experience of building, training, and testing predictive models. Beyond the technical skills, it made me appreciate the wider potential of machine learning. Similar approaches can be applied across industries, such as demand forecasting in logistics, pricing optimisation in retail, fraud detection in finance, and customer churn prediction in telecoms. The work also demonstrated how accessible machine learning has become through Python libraries, but at the same time, how important it is to go deeper than just applying pre-built models.

Looking ahead, I’m keen to:

  • Work on real-world, large-scale datasets where issues like missing data, feature engineering, and deployment are more complex.
  • Strengthen my grasp of the statistical foundations behind models (e.g., bias-variance trade-off, interpretability, error analysis) to ensure I don’t just “use a model” but understand why it works and its limitations.
  • Explore specialised models and architectures developed in industry and research - for example, deep learning approaches in image recognition or natural language processing - which go beyond standard regression/classification libraries.
  • Focus on business application: ensuring that models are not only technically accurate but also explainable and actionable for decision-makers.
