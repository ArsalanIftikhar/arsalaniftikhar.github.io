Premier League 21/22 — Shooting Data Analysis (Python)

A reproducible exploratory notebook analysing shot, xG and finishing performance across the 2021/22 Premier League season using Python, pandas and matplotlib.

Project Type

Data Analysis

Core Technologies

Python, pandas, matplotlib, seaborn

Domain

Sports Analytics

Impact

Performance Insights & Scouting

Overview

This notebook loads fbref-derived shooting data for the 2021/22 Premier League, cleans and filters players with a reasonable shot volume, and explores relationships between shots, xG and goals. Visual outputs (scatter plots, pies) illustrate positional differences, finishing efficiency and the link between shot distance and chance quality.

Loading & Cleaning

Data was ingested from an Excel export into a pandas DataFrame. Basic checks (row count, dtypes, descriptive statistics) confirmed the dataset integrity. For analysis we applied a volume filter to remove players with very low shot counts (to avoid noise from small-sample extremes).

Representative Python snippet


    # imports & load
    import pandas as pd
    import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
    import seaborn as sns

    epl_df = pd.read_excel('epl_data.xlsx')

    # filter: at least 15 shots and >0.7 shots per 90
    cln_df = epl_df[(epl_df['Sh'] >= 15) & (epl_df['Sh/90'] > 0.7)]

Exploration & Key Findings

Top performers & finishing efficiency

We computed G − xG for each player to identify over- and under-performers. The top scorers not only had high volume but collectively showed a positive G − xG, suggesting above-average finishing ability compared to the modelled expectation.

Average shot distance vs non-penalty xG/shot (negative slope); further shots = lower xG/shot

xG vs goals (positive relationship); xG vs goals shows tight correlation signalling xG validity

Positional & age breakdowns

Position-level aggregation shows forwards account for the largest share of shots, while midfielders form the largest group of players. Age-band charts indicate most players fall within the 24–29 bracket.

Conclusions & Practical Takeaways

  • xG is a reliable predictor: xG closely tracks goals across players — useful for scouting and performance evaluation.
  • Volume matters, but so does quality: top scorers combine high shot volume with superior finishing (positive G − xG).
  • Tactical applications: coaches can prioritise creating higher-quality chances (closer / central) and instruct defenders to force shots from distance to reduce opponent xG.
  • Limitations: league-level averages mask player-specific contexts; single-player longitudinal data would enable richer, actionable scouting insights.
