Project Overview

This project was completed as a technical assessment: the brief was to take multiple disconnected CSVs containing sales, product, customer and employee data and convert them into a single, reliable, and interactive Power BI report. The primary goals were to demonstrate robust data modelling (star schema), advanced DAX measures for business metrics, and a user-friendly report UX that enables rapid decision making.

The Business Challenge

Sales and commercial teams needed a single source of truth but data was spread across multiple CSV files (sales transactions, products, categories, customers, cities/countries, and employees). This fragmentation created a number of analytical pain points:

  • Difficulty understanding product mix and which SKUs drive the majority of revenue.
  • Hard to compare salesperson performance and spot coaching or incentive opportunities.
  • No quick way to measure the impact of discounting on realised revenue versus list price.
  • Time-consuming manual consolidation for month-over-month or YTD trend analysis.

My Solution: An Integrated Sales Reporting Dashboard

I delivered a compact, board-ready Power BI report built on a performant data model and expressive DAX measures. The approach focused on two pillars:

1. Data Modeling: The Star Schema

The backbone of the report is a star schema to ensure reliable measures and fast queries.

  • Fact Table: FactSales containing transactional rows (quantity, discount, transaction number, sales date).
  • Dimension Tables: DimProduct (Product + Category denormalised), DimCustomer (customer + city/country flattened), DimEmployee (salesperson), and a dedicated DimDate table for time intelligence.
  • Relationships: Clean, single-direction relationships from each dimension to the fact table to preserve predictable filter behavior and performance.

2. Dashboard Development

The front-end is designed for rapid insight discovery and drill-down analysis for commercial stakeholders.

  • KPI Cards: High-level metrics such as Total Sales, Orders, Avg Order Value (AOV), and Discount % are surfaced for immediate executive view.
  • Visualisations: Monthly trend lines, Top N product bars, Product → Category matrix with % of category, and Sales Team leaderboard help explain performance drivers.
  • Interactive Filters & Tooltips: Slicers for date range, category, price band and salesperson are synced across pages; report-page tooltips show this/last month deltas and top contributors on hover.

Data Model & Technical Implementation

To make the analytics robust and maintainable, I included clear technical artefacts: a visual model view showing the star schema, organised measure folders for governance, and a two-layer Power Query structure (staging + final queries). Measures Organisation

Measures are grouped into display folders (Core KPIs, Time Intelligence, Pricing & Discounting, Operational Throughput, Product Mix, Sales Team). This organisation helps stakeholders and future maintainers find and understand business logic faster.

Power Query: Staging & Final Queries

The queries pane demonstrates a two-layer approach: raw CSV imports (stg*) are used for initial cleansing and type-setting, and final referenced queries (Dim* / Fact*) are built on top for loading into the model. Staging queries are disabled for load to keep the model tidy while preserving lineage.

Key Features & Actionable Insights

  • Sales Health Overview: A single pane summarising revenue, order volume, average order value, and discount impact.
  • Product Mix & Concentration: Quickly identifies which categories and products contribute the most to revenue and where concentration risk exists.
  • Discount & Pricing Analysis: Contrast realised revenue against gross list value to quantify the exact impact of discounts and identify outlier behaviour by product or salesperson.
  • Sales Team Performance: Leaderboards, MoM deltas and throughput metrics (orders per day) enable targeted coaching and incentive design.
