Fabric migration notes: controls that protect reporting trust

Applied notes from migration work where the priority is stable reporting and governed metrics during platform change.

Where I apply this

Fabric modernisation workstreams moving legacy BI assets into layered models and governed semantic outputs.

What problem this avoids

Metric drift, logic duplication, and low confidence during transition from legacy reports.

Why it matters in practice

Operations need continuity; migration only succeeds if reporting remains trusted during and after cutover.

Delivery sequence I use

  • Scope first: lock outcomes, non-goals, and domain ownership before build.
  • Model before visuals: align grain, conformed keys, and semantic definitions early.
  • Controls early: reconciliation checks from source to transform to semantic output.
  • Release discipline: runbook, ownership map, and escalation path before go-live.

Design choices and trade-offs

  • Incremental migration over big-bang: slower, but lower operational reporting risk.
  • Layered architecture over quick patching: more setup effort, better long-term supportability.
  • Explicit KPI stewardship: upfront alignment cost, less downstream rework and debate.

Practical rule: if a migration decision cannot be traced to a metric-confidence outcome, revisit it.

High-value controls

  • Source-to-semantic reconciliation for critical metrics.
  • Exception thresholds with named owners and triage process.
  • Change log for KPI logic updates and release notes for downstream users.

What this demonstrates

Fabric modernisation is not just platform adoption; it is delivery discipline for trusted reporting, governed metrics, and reliable handover.
